Are you a teacher willing to start with Inquiry-based Learning?

Inquiry-based Learning (IBL) is not only a task for students. Teachers' guidance is crucial to design, implement and maintain the IBL process for students. In DojoIBL we offer a variety of resources to teachers that might be beneficial and helpful to begin, understand and grasp the IBL idea.

Our Team

Interdisciplinary team that join efforts, technical and pedagogical point of views

Dr. Stefaan Ternier

Dr. Stefaan Ternier is an assistant professor at Welten Institute. He received his degree in Computer Science in 2000 at K.U.Leuven where later in 2008 obtained a PhD. During this period his research topics included metadata, architectures for managing and sharing metadata, learning objects and standards for learning object repositories.

Since 2008, he works for the open university of the Netherland where he joined the mobile media group. From this moment onwards his research interests shifted towards mobile learning and augmented reality. As a result of this research, ARLearn - a toolkit for mobile serious games - was developed.

Technology-wise, Stefaan has experience with developing web services, cloud computing, database management systems and iOS/android developing.

Angel Suarez

Angel Suarez is PhD candidate at WELTEN institute of the Open Universiteit of the Netherlands. He received his diploma in IT (Technical Engineering) at Oviedo University in 2010. After that he started to work in a company for two years. On his second year at the company he started his master degree in Web Engineering at Oviedo University in 2011, which was ended in 2013 after present his final project at CELSTEC. Afterwards he joined this group to start his PhD entitled "Mobile Seamless Learning Support in Mixed Realities”.

Prof. Dr. Marcus Specht

Prof. Dr. Marcus Specht is Professor for Advanced Learning Technologies at Welten Institute (Research Center for Learning, Teaching and Technology) at the Open University of the Netherlands and director oft the Learning Innovation Labs. He received his Diploma in Psychology in 1995 and a Dissertation from the University of Trier in 1998 on adaptive information technology. From 2001 he headed the department "Mobile Knowledge" at the Fraunhofer Institute for Applied Information Technology (FIT). His research focus is on Mobile and Contextualized Learning Technologies and Social and Immersive Media for Learning. Since 2014 he is Program Chair Technology Enhanced Learning Innovations at the Welten Institute and member of the management team of the Welten Institute. Prof. Specht is a member of ACM, IEEE, the SIKS and ICO research schools in the Netherlands and is an Apple Distinguished Educator and since 2013 President of the International Association of Mobile Learning.

Dr. Fleur Prinsen

Dr. Fleur R. Prinsen is Assistant professor at Open University the Netherlands, working in the domain of learning technologies and (in)formal learning, with an emphasis on 'Online Social Learning' and Networked Learning. She completed her Master in Educational Psychology at the University of Leiden (2001) and received her PhD on the topic of differences in CSCL at the Vrije Universiteit of Amsterdam (2007). She conducted postdoctoral research at the Institute for Knowledge Innovation Technology at OISE (University of Toronto), at the National Institute of Education in Singapore and at Utrecht University. Her research has been globally concerned with social learning processes, enhanced by (social) media use. Supporting learner differences has always been a focus therein. In terms of learning domains her research is concerned with science learning and developing attitudes for active, self-directed, exploratory learning.

Specialities: (Interactive) media use for learning, differences in CSCL participation related to learning outcomes, the Collective Nature of Science (learning), networked learning, identity development in (online) social networks, network analysis, practice based research.

DojoIBL roadmap

We like transparency and we like to do the right things. With this timeline we want to show you from where we are coming from and to where we are aiming at. By showing you our our way of working you will be able to tune faster and better with our work.

Kick-off meeting

The weSPOT project is ended but we want to continue the work and also we want to put in practice all the lessons learned. Therefore we started the design of DojoIBL as follow up initiative. In this kick-off meeting the workflow, the architecture and the basic requirements have been discussed.

Meeting with teachers

Our idea is to develop the platform for teachers and students. Thus we want to involved as much as possible teachers on the process. With that idea in mind we have started a series of talks with teachers doing Inquiry-based Learning. We are planning to work with them on iterations. Therefore more teachers and meetings will come to continue improving. The Agora school in Roermond, represented by one its teachers, agreed on taking part of this process.
